Union Eligibility & Organizing

what does union representation mean?

What does union representation mean?

  • The union becomes the exclusive representative of covered employees, and everything related to their wages, hours, and other terms and conditions of employment (including health and retirement benefits, time off, promotions, discipline, etc.) would have to be negotiated between the Museum and the union, and formally outlined in a labor contract.

  • Under Federal law, the Museum would be prohibited from dealing directly with covered employees in connection with any of their wages, hours, and terms and conditions of employment.

  • The union contract would apply to all employees included in the bargaining unit, regardless of whether they voted in favor of the union or not. Once a union contract is signed, whether a covered employee agrees with it or not, it applies to them.

WHat is the field museum's position on unionization?

What is the Field Museum's position on unionization?

We fully respect our employees’ right to decide whether or not to form and join a union. We do not, however, view unionization as the best path forward for our museum community. We believe that both the Museum and our employees benefit most when we can work directly with each other rather than through a third-party union unfamiliar with the Museum’s mission, culture, and community. The Field Museum has been and remains committed to cultivating its relationship with our employees, keeping the lines of communication open, and finding the best pathway forward to creating a positive, collaborative, and equitable work environment.

who is excluded from joining a union?

Who is excluded from joining a union?

  • The National Labor Relations Act specifically excludes managers (those who use independent discretion to set and implement management policies) and supervisors (those who hire, lay off, discipline, responsibly assign and direct, etc., or those who effectively recommend such actions), as well as a few additional classifications of employees.

  • Managers and supervisors cannot join unions or be part of the bargaining unit. They are considered to be part of a company's management rather than its labor force.

  • Security guards may not be included in the same unit with other employees.

Who is eligible to be in the union?

Who is eligible to be in the union?

As a general matter, any employees who do not meet the definition of a manager, supervisor, confidential employee, or temporary employee under the National Labor Relations Act are eligible to form or join a union.

  • Managers are those who set and implement management policies, including academic programming.

  • Supervisors are those who use their own discretion and judgment to responsibly assign and direct the work of others or who have the authority to hire, fire, discipline, promote, transfer, reward, layoff, recall or adjust employee grievances – or to effectively recommend such action – for other employees.

  • Confidential employees are those who assist and act in a confidential capacity to the management personnel who make and implement labor relations policies or as someone who has regular access to confidential information about future bargaining strategy or changes that the employer anticipates may result from collective bargaining.

  • Temporary employees are those whose employment is finite, and its end is reasonably ascertainable, and they do not have a reasonable expectation of reemployment.

These are fact-specific analyses, but we have communicated with the employees at the Museum, who we believe are managers, supervisors, and confidential employees.

When will we know if we are personally eligible to be in the union and who decides who is eligible?

When will we know if we are personally eligible to be in the union and who decides who is eligible?

The Museum has communicated with employees it believes are managers, supervisors, and confidential employees. The more official process leading to determinations on these issues will begin with the filing of a petition for recognition with the National Labor Relations Board. In that filing, the petitioning union will identify the “bargaining unit” with a description of the employees it seeks to represent. Once that is filed, the Museum and the union will confer about the proposed unit and attempt to reach agreement on eligible voters. If disagreements remain between the parties, and the number of disputed voters is less than 20% of the proposed unit, those individuals will likely be allowed to vote subject to challenge by either the union or the Museum, and the issue will either be resolved after the vote by the parties or in a hearing where the union and the Museum present evidence to the NLRB. The NLRB will decide on the issue based on the evidence. If the amount of disputed voters is greater than 20% of the total proposed unit, then there will likely be a hearing before the vote, where the union and the Museum present evidence, and the NLRB will decide.

what is collective bargaining?

What is collective bargaining?

  • If an election is held and concludes with an outcome to unionize, collective bargaining is the process that requires an employer and the union to meet at reasonable times and confer in good faith about mandatory subjects of bargaining—such as pay, benefits, and other conditions of employment.

  • The employer, however, is not required to agree to any particular contract provision proposed by the union or to make concessions.

  • The law does not require the employer to continue its present benefits in any contract negotiated with the union; whatever compensation and benefits employees receive after unionization will have to be negotiated.

  • There are no guarantees in collective bargaining, and if a union is voted in, the compensation and benefits employees receive could end up being less, more, or the same as currently received.

when can employees solicit support for the union?

When can employees solicit support for the union?

  • Employees may only engage in solicitation and distribution related to unionization during non-working time (e.g., during breaks, meal periods, before/after clocking in).

  • Distribution can also only occur in non-work areas (e.g., break rooms, parking lots, etc.).

Isn’t it illegal for managers to discuss our unionizing efforts with us?

Isn’t it illegal for managers to discuss our unionizing efforts with us?

No. Since the 1940s, the U.S. Supreme Court has recognized that an employer has the right to engage in non-coercive speech about unionization with its employees, and 1947 amendments to the National Labor Relations Act manifest a “congressional intent to encourage free debate on issues dividing labor and management.” Under this legal authority, employers are allowed to share information about unionization, express opinions on unionization, and share union experiences with employees; it also permits an employer to talk with employees regarding the employer’s view about why it disfavors unionization in its unique workplace.

what does it mean if someone asks me to sign a union authorization card?

What does it mean is someone asks me to sign a union authorization card?


  • Union authorization cards (also known as union cards or election cards) are used to collect signatures in support of unionizing and are an initial step of unionization. A colleague or union representative may ask you to sign one of these cards, which could look like a petition or a form and could be paper or online.

  • Signing an authorization card or a petition does not just mean you are “interested” or “curious.” It means that you are in support of a union and you are signaling to the union you want them to be your exclusive representative. It is a legally binding document.

if i signed a union authorization card, does that mean i have no choice but to vote for the union if it goes to an election?

If I signed a union authorization card, does that mean I have no choice but to vote for the union if it goes to an election?

  • No. If there is a union election, all votes are confidential, and you will be completely free to cast your vote however you see fit at that time, regardless of whether or not you signed a union authorization card. It is not necessary to sign a card in order to vote in the election.

  • However, if you don’t want a union, you should not sign a union authorization card in the first place. Signing such a card is a signal to the union that you want them to represent you.

what if i already signed a union card and want to revoke it? can i do that?

What if I already signed a union card and want to revoke it? Can I do that?

  • Yes. It is illegal for a union to restrict your right to revoke a union authorization card that you signed.

  • You may revoke any union authorization card you have signed by notifying AFSCME Council 31 and your manager or Judith Swedek, Executive Director of Human Resources, at jswedek@fieldmuseum.org, in writing that you do not support the union, you do not want the union to be your representative, that you do not want to be a member of the union, and that you are specifically and unequivocally revoking the authorization you previously signed. You can ask that your authorization card, and all copies of it, be returned to you or ask for confirmation in writing from the union that it is honoring the revocation of your support for the union.

can i opt out of the union by not voting?

Can I opt out of the union by not voting?

The outcome of a union election is determined by the majority of those who vote, not the majority of those eligible to vote. Even if only a small number of eligible employees actually vote, the union will be certified if a majority of the vote is “yes.”

if i don't vote in the election and a union gets voted in, am i still covered by the collective bargaining agreement?

If I don't vote in the election and a union gets voted in, am I still covered by the collective bargaining agreement?

Yes, if a union is voted in, all employees in the collective bargaining unit are subject to the terms of the collective bargaining agreement regardless of whether they voted in the election.

if a union represents me, do I have to pay union dues?

If a union represents me, do I have to pay union dues?

Yes. Even if you did not support unionization, if a union is voted in, you will be required to pay union dues. Also, if the union negotiates a “union security clause”—which they commonly do—you could possibly face termination of employment if you do not pay union dues.

If AFSCME were voted in, would employees be required to participate in the union?

If AFSCME were voted in, would employees be required to participate in the union?

Whether union membership is compulsory is a subject for negotiation at the bargaining table. Based on what we know about AFSCME Council 31’s bargaining position in the private sector, we anticipate that they would seek what is called a “union security” clause. Those clauses typically contain two key components: (a) the requirement of membership (or fee paying status) in the union; and (b) automatic deduction of dues or fees from your paycheck. Ask AFSCME if they intend to propose a union security clause at the bargaining table if the union is voted in. You are entitled to know.

in the future, what if field museum employees want to stop working with a union? can we do that?

In the future, what if Field Museum employees want to stop working with a union? Can we do that?

  • Everyone should be aware that you cannot just “give the union a try.” Once a union is voted in, it is very difficult to end the relationship. This process is called decertification, and in the past five years, only 33% of attempts at decertification across the United States have been successful.

  • Even if you withdraw from the union individually, the union is still the bargaining representative and has the exclusive authority to negotiate your wages, benefits, and other important employment terms.
